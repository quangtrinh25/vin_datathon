\begin{abstract}
Accurate retail revenue forecasting is crucial for supply chain stability and marketing allocation. This paper presents an end-to-end forecasting pipeline that identifies a critical structural anomaly---a biennial margin collapse driven by odd-year inventory liquidations. By combining prescriptive exploratory data analysis with an optimized Non-Negative Least Squares (NNLS) hybrid forecasting ensemble, we establish a deterministic Holt-Winters fallback that ensures absolute reproducibility while capturing volatile holiday demand spikes.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

In retail apparel, demand volatility and rigid procurement often force catastrophic margin erosion. Predicting revenue and COGS is fundamentally a supply chain and cash-flow preservation problem. This paper bridges diagnostic analytics and predictive forecasting by conducting an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) across four core dashboards to uncover structural anomalies, followed by a robust machine learning pipeline to forecast daily metrics. Section 2 presents the EDA. Section 3 details the forecasting pipeline, including SHAP feature importance. Finally, Section 4 reports our quantitative results.

\section{Forecasting Model}

\subsection{Problem Framing}
The objective is to predict daily Revenue and COGS across an 18-month horizon (Jan 2023--Jul 2024). The pipeline operates under strict deterministic reproducibility constraints, ensuring identical execution across environments. No future-leaking features or external data were utilized.

\subsection{Feature Engineering}
To capture macroeconomic seasonality, we extracted cyclic calendar features and encoded Fourier terms. We engineered deep rolling statistics (7-day to 90-day lags) to capture momentum, alongside boolean flags for major events (e.g., Liberation Day) to anticipate impulse demand. To prevent leakage, all rolling features were computed within a strict shift-window, ensuring historical targets were never contaminated by future data.

\subsection{Pipeline}
Our forecasting pipeline is a 3-Tier ensemble. The foundational layer utilizes Holt-Winters exponential smoothing [4] to anchor ML models against hallucination. The second layer trains specialized LightGBM [1], XGBoost [2], and Ridge models on quarterly splits. Finally, these sub-models are blended using Non-Negative Least Squares (NNLS) [5] for optimal weights. Cross-validation used a Walk-Forward Time-Series Split to prevent time-shuffling leakage. To guarantee reproducibility, we disabled L-BFGS dependent libraries (\texttt{HAS\_PROPHET = False}) in favor of the deterministic Holt-Winters fallback.

The \texttt{day\_of\_year} and \texttt{month} features are the absolute strongest predictors. Conversely, the absence of promotional flags (blue dots) strongly suppresses the baseline forecast, pulling predictions downward. Interestingly, while the 30-day rolling mean provides short-term momentum context, the boolean holiday flags proved highly impactful for predicting isolated, high-magnitude impulse days. Finally, the COGS SHAP summary perfectly mirrors the Revenue summary, validating that our pipeline successfully learned the fundamental, locked relationship between inventory cost and top-line volume.

\section{Conclusion}

The exploratory data analysis revealed that top-line revenue growth is an illusion if it is negated by the self-inflicted biennial margin trap. By flattening the odd-year August inventory clearance cycle, the business can immediately rescue millions in gross profit that are currently lost to forced markdowns.

Our forecasting pipeline leverages this structural understanding. The NNLS-blended ensemble of LightGBM, XGBoost, and Holt-Winters anchors the macroeconomic growth while reacting dynamically to holiday spikes, ultimately driven by engineered temporal features as verified by our SHAP explainability analysis.

While highly accurate for macroeconomic prediction, the pipeline lacks SKU-level granularity. The next step is decentralizing the global model into category-specific micro-models, allowing procurement to order Streetwear and GenZ inventory on completely isolated predictive timelines.
